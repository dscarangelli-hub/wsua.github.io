About This Hub

The WSUA Legal Research Hub brings together work on reparations, restitution, transitional justice, and property rights in wartime and post‑war settings. It is designed as a living space for research, guidance, and resources that support Ukraine’s pursuit of justice and long‑term recovery.

This page highlights key documents, frameworks, and ongoing projects. As our work evolves, we will continue to update this hub with new findings, tools, and guidance.

Core Research Areas

  • Reparations & Restitution — comparative research on international reparations models, compensation schemes, and community‑based restitution.
  • Transitional Justice — frameworks for accountability, truth‑seeking, institutional reform, and victim participation in post‑conflict settings.
  • Property Rights in Wartime & Post‑War Contexts — legal protections for displaced people, destroyed property, and contested land.
  • Claims Guidance & Access to Justice — tools and guidance to help individuals and communities understand potential claims processes in future reparations or restitution mechanisms.

Current Focus: Claims Framework Mapping

WSUA is currently examining comparative claims mechanisms from other post‑conflict contexts to identify potential models relevant to Ukraine. This includes administrative reparations programs, hybrid tribunals, and community‑based restitution initiatives.

Upcoming Work

  • Drafting a public‑facing explainer on reparations and restitution for affected communities.
  • Developing a structured database of property loss categories and potential claims pathways.
  • Exploring partnerships with legal clinics and pro‑bono networks.
